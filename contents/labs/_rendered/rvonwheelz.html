RVonWheelz

A carpooling app built for a single residential community in Hyderabad. Restricted scope is the entire design choice.

Why scope is the whole story

Carpooling apps usually fail because they need a critical mass of users in a wide geography. Within one residential society, that critical mass already exists, and the route patterns are predictable.

Restricting the scope to one society means:

  • Identity verification is trivial (the society already has membership records)
  • Trust is already established
  • Routes converge — most people are going to similar nearby destinations
  • The product can ship at <1% of the complexity of a public carpool app

What this lab is for

It's a study in scope as a design tool. The interesting question isn't "how do we scale to millions" — it's "how small can we keep the product while still being useful?" In this case: small enough to ship by one person, on weekends.
